An Italian treat waiting to be savoured - Pine Nut Cookies

Why you should try pine nut cookies!

If you are looking for a cookie that is nutty, sweet, and rich, you'll appreciate the pine nut cookie. This gourmet delicacy well worth exploring is known as pignoli or pignolate in the italian tradition. If you have ever craved a treat that is luxurious yet homey, this cookie might be worth trying.

Taste disturbance warning!!

    It's a bitter truth that pine nuts can cause some people to experience a metallic or bitter taste, a disturbance known as the 'pine mouth' syndrome. This bitter aftertaste, although rare, may begin 12 hours after consuming pine nuts and last from a few days up to several weeks. Thousands of cases have been reported by people describing that their sense of taste has been distorted, diminished or even dissapeared for a long period of time (about several weeks), resulting in a loss of apetite and the onset of full on nuttiness.

Considerations

  • Gluten-free: Pine nut cookies don't actually need wheat flour as is the case for the classic pignoli recipes which omit flour altogether. Since they rely on almond paste and egg whites as binders, these biscuits can naturally be prepared gluten-free.

Tips

Store pine nut brittle in an airtight container at room temperature. Use it as a snack, gift, or topping for desserts like ice cream or yogurt.

Nutritional benefits

Although there isn't one healthiest nut to consume, they are all rich in healthy fats that are good for the heart, loads of antioxidants, and full of nutrients.
